# Tomislav Wittenberg

## About

Tomislav Josip Wittenberg (Thomas Joseph Wittenberg), born on January 8, 1937, in Baæur, was a prolific Croatian writer, historian, and editor, recognized for his significant contributions to the cultural and historical preservation of the Požega region. He passed away on October 2, 2020, in Požega.

Wittenberg’s extensive body of work, often written under the pseudonym Stipa iz Puvarije, left an indelible mark on the local and broader Croatian cultural landscape. He was awarded the Grad Požega Nagrada za životno djelo (Lifetime Achievement Award) on March 12, 2016, acknowledging his lifelong dedication to documenting and preserving regional history.

Born into a family deeply rooted in the area, Tomislav’s early years were shaped by the rural landscape and the socio-political upheavals of the 20th century. His mother, Delfina Moretti, was of Italian descent, born in Martinazzu, Italy, in 1908, while his father, Slavko, raised Tomislav alongside his brothers Ivan and Branko. Wittenberg’s educational journey began during the tumultuous years of World War II, and he later pursued a career in education, politics, and public service, which he enriched with his lifelong passion for history and literature.

After his retirement, Tomislav dedicated himself fully to writing. He authored numerous monographs, each a testament to his commitment to recording the stories of local communities, historical landmarks, and the cultural heritage of the Požega region. Among his notable works are Groblja Požeške Doline (1996), Puvarija (1998), 100 Godina DVD Ruševo (2003), and Brestovac – zapadna vrata Požeštine (2011), showcasing his deep connection to the region’s history and people.

He also contributed to scientific conferences and collaborated on publications that explored the history of German and Austrian settlers in Croatia, underlining his scholarly breadth. His articles, such as Doseljavanje Nijemaca u središnji dio Požeške doline (2002) and Johann Nepomuk Scholl 1828-1908 (2006), were published in the Godišnjak VDG annuals, further demonstrating his historical expertise.

Wittenberg’s editorial work included overseeing significant local publications like the monograph Trenkovo po Trenku – Mitrovica po Mitru (2008) and the Smotra magazine of the Croatian-German Society Zagreb (2012).

His lifelong commitment to documenting the history of Požega’s landscapes, people, and cultural intersections ensures that his work will continue to be a vital resource for historians, researchers, and anyone interested in Croatia's rich cultural history.
